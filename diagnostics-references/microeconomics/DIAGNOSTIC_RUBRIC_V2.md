## SECTION I: MULTIPLE CHOICE ANSWER KEY (10 points)

**Instructions:** Each correct answer earns 1 point. No partial credit.

### Question 1: **C** - Elastic Demand

**Calculation:**
$$\text{PED} = \frac{\%\Delta Q_d}{\%\Delta P} = \frac{(80-100)/90}{(12-10)/11} = \frac{-0.222}{0.182} = -1.22$$

Since $|PED| > 1$, demand is **elastic**.

**CED Reference:** Unit 2 (MKT-2.F) - Calculate and interpret price elasticity

---

### Question 2: **B** - Price = Marginal Cost

**Explanation:**
In perfect competition:
- Firms are price takers: P = MR
- Profit maximization: MR = MC
- Therefore: **P = MR = MC**

**Common Error:** Confusing profit maximization (P = MC) with break-even point (P = ATC)

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-1.A) - Perfect competition characteristics

---

### Question 3: **A** - Marginal Revenue = Marginal Cost

**Explanation:**
For ALL profit-maximizing firms (monopoly, monopolistic competition, perfect competition):
- **MR = MC** determines quantity

Key difference for monopoly:
- MR < P (downward-sloping demand)
- Firm charges P > MC (from demand curve)

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-2.B) - Monopoly profit maximization

---

### Question 4: **C** - Less output at a higher price

**Explanation:**
Monopoly vs Perfect Competition:
- **Quantity:** Monopoly produces where MR = MC (less than competitive Q where P = MC)
- **Price:** Monopoly charges price from demand curve (higher than competitive P)

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-2.C) - Compare market structures

---

### Question 5: **B** - Produces less than socially optimal quantity

**Explanation:**
Deadweight loss occurs because:
- Socially optimal: Q where P = MC (competitive quantity)
- Monopoly produces: Q where MR = MC (less than optimal)
- Gap between these quantities = deadweight loss

**Note:** Answer A is TRUE but doesn't explain WHY deadweight loss occurs.

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-2.D) - Deadweight loss

---

### Question 6: **B** - Overproduce relative to socially optimal

**Explanation:**
Negative externality (e.g., pollution):
- Marginal Social Cost (MSC) > Marginal Private Cost (MPC)
- Market produces where P = MPC
- Socially optimal: produce where P = MSC
- Market overproduces

**CED Reference:** Unit 4 (MSC-1.A) - Externalities

---

### Question 7: **D** - Price equal to average total cost

**Explanation:**
Monopolistic competition long-run equilibrium:
- New firms enter if profit > 0
- Demand curve shifts left until P = ATC
- Zero economic profit: $\pi = (P - ATC) \times Q = 0$

**Note:** Does NOT produce at minimum ATC (that's perfect competition)

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-2.C) - Monopolistic competition

---

### Question 8: **C** - Significant barriers to entry

**Explanation:**
Perfect competition characteristics:
- ✓ Many buyers and sellers
- ✓ Homogeneous product
- ✓ Price-taking behavior
- ✗ **NO barriers to entry** (free entry/exit)

Significant barriers = monopoly/oligopoly, not perfect competition

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-1.A) - Market structures

---

### Question 9: **B** - Decrease production to maximize profit

**Explanation:**
If MC > MR:
- Last unit produced costs more than revenue it generates
- Firm is producing too much
- Should decrease Q until MR = MC

**Rule:** Produce where MR = MC, not beyond it

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-1.B) - Profit maximization

---

### Question 10: **C** - Increase in consumer income

**Explanation:**
For a **normal good**:
- Income ↑ → Demand ↑ (positive relationship)

Other options:
- A: Income ↓ → Demand ↓ (decrease, not increase)
- B: Complement price ↑ → Demand ↓ (complementary relationship)
- D: Fewer consumers → Demand ↓ (market demand decreases)

**CED Reference:** Unit 2 (MKT-1.C) - Determinants of demand

## SECTION II: GRAPH-BASED SHORT RESPONSE RUBRIC (10 points)

**Scenario:** Monopolistically competitive firm (FreshBite) with positive short-run profit

**Graph Key Information:**
- MR = MC at Q = 40
- At Q = 40: P = $20, ATC = $15
- Minimum ATC at Q = 60, ATC = $12

---

### Part A: Identify Profit-Maximizing Q and P (3 points)

**Question:** Identify profit-maximizing quantity and price + explain economic reasoning

**Scoring Rubric:**

| Points | Criterion |
|--------|-----------|
| **1 pt** | **Identifies Q = 40 units** |
| **1 pt** | **Identifies P = $20** |
| **1 pt** | **Explains reasoning:** "The firm maximizes profit where MR = MC, which occurs at Q = 40. At this quantity, the firm charges the price consumers are willing to pay from the demand curve, which is $20." |

**Acceptable variations:**
- "MR = MC rule determines profit-maximizing quantity"
- "Price comes from demand curve at Q = 40"
- "Firm produces where marginal revenue equals marginal cost"

**Common Errors:**
- ❌ "P = $15" (confusing ATC with price)
- ❌ "Q = 60" (confusing minimum ATC with profit max)
- ❌ "P = MC" (confusing with perfect competition)
- ❌ Not explaining WHY Q = 40 and P = $20

**Partial Credit:**
- 2/3: Correct Q and P, but weak or missing explanation
- 1/3: Only Q or P correct, no explanation

---

### Part B: Calculate Total Economic Profit (4 points)

**Question:** Calculate total profit, show all work

**Scoring Rubric:**

| Points | Criterion |
|--------|-----------|
| **1 pt** | **States profit formula:** Profit = (P - ATC) × Q |
| **1 pt** | **Calculates per-unit profit:** $20 - $15 = $5 per unit |
| **1 pt** | **Calculates total profit:** $5 × 40 = $200 |
| **1 pt** | **Shows clear work:** All steps labeled and organized |

**Complete Answer Example:**
```
Profit = (P - ATC) × Q
      = ($20 - $15) × 40
      = $5 × 40
      = $200

FreshBite earns a total economic profit of $200.
```

**Common Errors:**
- ❌ Using MC instead of ATC: (P - MC) × Q
- ❌ Only calculating per-unit profit: $5 (forgetting to multiply by Q)
- ❌ Calculating (P - ATC) but not multiplying by quantity
- ❌ No work shown (just stating "$200")

**Partial Credit:**
- 3/4: Correct formula and per-unit profit, arithmetic error in final answer
- 2/4: Correct formula, error in per-unit profit calculation
- 1/4: Attempts formula but uses wrong cost curve (MC instead of ATC)
- 0/4: No formula, random number, or uses revenue instead of profit

### Part C: Long-Run Market Adjustment (3 points)

**Question:** Explain long-run changes: entry/exit, demand curve, economic profit

**Scoring Rubric:**

| Points | Criterion |
|--------|-----------|
| **1 pt** | **Entry/Exit:** "New firms will enter the market because existing firms are earning positive economic profit ($200 > 0), which attracts entrepreneurs." |
| **1 pt** | **Demand Curve:** "As new firms enter, FreshBite's demand curve will shift to the LEFT because new restaurants capture some of FreshBite's customers and market share." |
| **1 pt** | **Long-Run Profit:** "Entry continues until economic profit reaches zero, which occurs when P = ATC at the profit-maximizing quantity (where MR = MC)." |

**Key Phrases Required:**
- "NEW firms ENTER" (not exit)
- "Demand shifts LEFT" (not right, not just "decreases")
- "P = ATC" (specific long-run condition)
- "Zero economic profit" (not negative profit)

**Economic Reasoning Required:**
- WHY firms enter (positive profit signal)
- HOW entry affects demand (competition for customers)
- WHEN entry stops (zero profit condition)

**Common Errors:**

**Part 1 - Entry/Exit:**
- ❌ "Firms will exit" (wrong direction - positive profit attracts entry)
- ❌ "Firms enter because ATC is low" (wrong reason - it's about profit)
- ❌ Just stating "firms enter" without explaining why

**Part 2 - Demand Curve:**
- ❌ "Demand shifts right" (wrong direction)
- ❌ "Demand decreases" (vague - must specify LEFT shift)
- ❌ Not explaining WHY demand shifts (new competition)

**Part 3 - Long-Run Profit:**
- ❌ "Firms will earn losses" (no - zero profit, not losses)
- ❌ "P = MC" (wrong condition - that's perfect competition)
- ❌ "P < ATC" (wrong - would cause exit)
- ❌ Not stating the specific P = ATC condition

**Partial Credit:**
- 2/3: Correct entry and demand shift, weak explanation of long-run equilibrium
- 1/3: Only identifies entry correctly, other parts wrong or missing
- 0/3: Major conceptual errors (firms exit, demand shifts right, etc.)

## COMMON STUDENT ERRORS - BY CATEGORY

### Graph Interpretation Errors
1. ❌ **Confusing curves:** Using ATC value as price, or MC value as quantity
2. ❌ **Wrong intersection:** Using minimum ATC instead of MR = MC intersection
3. ❌ **Misreading coordinates:** Stating wrong numerical values from graph

### Calculation Errors
1. ❌ **Wrong formula:** Using (P - MC) instead of (P - ATC) for profit
2. ❌ **Forgetting quantity:** Only calculating per-unit profit
3. ❌ **Arithmetic mistakes:** Correct setup but calculation errors

### Reasoning Errors
1. ❌ **Memorization without understanding:** Stating facts without explaining WHY
2. ❌ **Confusing market structures:** Applying perfect competition rules to monopolistic competition
3. ❌ **Backwards logic:** "Firms exit" when there's positive profit

### Justification Errors
1. ❌ **No economic reasoning:** Just stating answers without explanation
2. ❌ **Circular reasoning:** "Q = 40 because that's the answer"
3. ❌ **Missing causation:** Not explaining WHY things happen (e.g., why firms enter)
