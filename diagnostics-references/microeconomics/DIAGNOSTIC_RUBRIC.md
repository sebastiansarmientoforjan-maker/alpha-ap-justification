## SECTION I: Multiple Choice Answer Key (5 points)

**Instructions:** Each correct answer earns 1 point. No partial credit.

### Question 1
**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
Price elasticity of demand (PED) is calculated as:

$$\text{PED} = \frac{\%\Delta Q_d}{\%\Delta P}$$

Using the midpoint method:
$$\text{PED} = \frac{(Q_2 - Q_1)/[(Q_2 + Q_1)/2]}{(P_2 - P_1)/[(P_2 + P_1)/2]}$$

$$= \frac{(80 - 100)/90}{(12 - 10)/11} = \frac{-20/90}{2/11} = \frac{-0.222}{0.182} \approx -1.22$$

Since $|PED| > 1$, demand is **elastic**.

**CED Reference:** Unit 2 (MKT-2.F) - Calculate and interpret price elasticity of demand

### Question 2
**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
In **perfect competition**, firms are price takers and produce where:
- **P = MR = MC**
- Long-run economic profit = 0
- P = minimum ATC in long run

At the profit-maximizing quantity, **P = MC**, not P = ATC (which occurs at the break-even point).

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-1.A) - Characteristics of perfect competition

---

### Question 3
**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
A **monopoly** maximizes profit where **MR = MC**, NOT where P = MC.

Key differences from perfect competition:
- Monopoly: P > MR (downward-sloping demand)
- Perfect competition: P = MR (horizontal demand)

Therefore:
- Monopoly produces where MR = MC
- Monopoly charges P > MC (creating deadweight loss)

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-2.B) - Monopoly profit maximization

---

### Question 4
**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
**Deadweight loss** occurs when market quantity is not at the socially optimal level (where demand = supply).

In a monopoly:
- Socially optimal: Q where D = MC
- Monopoly produces: Q where MR = MC
- Since MR < P, monopoly underproduces
- DWL = loss in total surplus from underproduction

**CED Reference:** Unit 3 (PRF-2.D) - Calculate and explain deadweight loss

---

### Question 5
**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
A **negative externality** occurs when:
- Private cost < Social cost
- Market overproduces (Q_market > Q_social)
- Marginal social cost (MSC) > Marginal private cost (MPC)

Example: Factory pollution
- Factory only considers private costs (wages, materials)
- Society bears additional costs (pollution, health effects)
- MSC = MPC + external cost

**CED Reference:** Unit 4 (MSC-1.A) - Identify and explain externalities

## SECTION II: Free-Response Scoring Rubric (9 points)

**Problem Statement:**

The graph below shows the demand (D), marginal revenue (MR), marginal cost (MC), and average total cost (ATC) curves for a monopolistically competitive firm in the short run.

[Assume a standard monopolistic competition graph where:
- Demand curve is downward-sloping
- MR is below D
- MC intersects MR at Q = 40
- At Q = 40: P = $20, ATC = $15
- MC curve intersects ATC at its minimum]

---

### Part (a): Profit-Maximizing Output and Price (3 points)

**Question:** Identify the profit-maximizing quantity and price for this firm. Explain how you determined these values.

**Scoring Rubric:**

| Points | Criterion |
|--------|-----------|
| **1 pt** | **Identifies profit-maximizing quantity:** States "Q = 40 units" |
| **1 pt** | **Identifies profit-maximizing price:** States "P = $20" |
| **1 pt** | **Explains the rule:** States "The firm maximizes profit where MR = MC. At this quantity, the firm charges the price consumers are willing to pay on the demand curve." |

**Notes:**
- Must explicitly state MR = MC rule
- Must explain that price comes from the demand curve, NOT the MR curve
- Common error: stating P = MR (confusing with perfect competition)

---

### Part (b): Economic Profit Calculation (3 points)

**Question:** Calculate the firm's total economic profit or loss. Show your work.

**Scoring Rubric:**

| Points | Criterion |
|--------|-----------|
| **1 pt** | **Calculates profit per unit:** $(P - ATC) = $20 - $15 = $5$ |
| **1 pt** | **Calculates total profit:** $\text{Profit} = (P - ATC) \times Q = $5 \times 40 = $200$ |
| **1 pt** | **Shows complete work:** Must show both per-unit profit and multiplication by quantity |

**Notes:**
- Must use ATC (not MC) to calculate profit
- Must multiply by quantity
- Common error: calculating (P - MC) instead of (P - ATC)

---

### Part (c): Long-Run Adjustment (3 points)

**Question:** Explain what will happen to this firm in the long run. What will happen to the number of firms, the firm's demand curve, and the firm's economic profit?

**Scoring Rubric:**

| Points | Criterion |
|--------|-----------|
| **1 pt** | **Entry of new firms:** States "New firms will enter the market because existing firms are earning positive economic profit" |
| **1 pt** | **Demand curve shifts:** States "The firm's demand curve will shift left (and become more elastic) as new firms enter and capture market share" |
| **1 pt** | **Long-run equilibrium:** States "Entry continues until economic profit = 0, where P = ATC at the profit-maximizing quantity" |

**Notes:**
- Must explain WHY firms enter (positive profit as signal)
- Must explain direction of demand shift (left, not just "decreases")
- Must state the specific long-run equilibrium condition: P = ATC
- Common error: saying "firms leave" instead of "firms enter"
- Common error: not explaining why demand shifts (new competition)

## Common Student Errors

### MCQ #2
❌ **"Firms produce where P = ATC"** - No, profit maximization is at P = MC (where MR = MC in perfect competition)

### MCQ #3
❌ **"Monopoly produces where P = MC"** - No, monopoly produces where MR = MC, then charges P > MC

### Part (b)
❌ **Using MC instead of ATC** - Profit per unit = (P - ATC), NOT (P - MC)
❌ **Forgetting to multiply by quantity** - Must calculate total profit, not just per-unit profit

### Part (c)
❌ **"Firms leave the market"** - No, positive profit attracts NEW firms (entry, not exit)
❌ **"Demand shifts right"** - No, new competition causes demand to shift LEFT
❌ **Not stating P = ATC condition** - Must explicitly state the long-run equilibrium condition
