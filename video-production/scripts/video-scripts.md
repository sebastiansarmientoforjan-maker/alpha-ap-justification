## AB Week 1: Applying Mean Value Theorem

**Duration:** 10 minutes
**Visuals:** ab-week1.png (infographic)

**SCRIPT:**
Welcome to Week 1 of AP Calculus AB justification training.

Today you will learn how to correctly apply the Mean Value Theorem.

The Mean Value Theorem has two required conditions. First, the function must be continuous on the closed interval from a to b. Second, the function must be differentiable on the open interval from a to b.

Many students skip directly to the calculation without verifying these conditions. This is a critical mistake.

Let's look at an example. Consider the function f of x equals absolute value of x on the interval from negative 1 to 1.

[PAUSE - Show infographic section on MVT conditions]

Can you apply the Mean Value Theorem here? Most students would calculate the average rate of change and try to solve for c. But there's a problem.

The function absolute value of x is NOT differentiable at x equals zero. The derivative does not exist at this point because there is a sharp corner.

[PAUSE - Show graph with cusp highlighted]

Therefore, the Mean Value Theorem does NOT apply to this function on this interval.

Your task in Week 1 is to verify BOTH conditions before applying any theorem. Check continuity first, then check differentiability.

Let's practice with another example...

[Continue with worked example from infographic]

Remember: Always verify conditions BEFORE applying theorems. This is the foundation of rigorous mathematical reasoning.

You'll practice this with error-forcing problems this week. Good luck!
